Candide

Voltaire

An entertaining satire of optimism and social institutions. A bit too hyperbolic at times.

In Cold Blood

Truman Capote

Capote is one of my favorite authors. His writing style is beautiful and heartbreaking. This book in particular contains a profound commentary on nature vs nuture, and an early depiction of the correlation betweeen head injuries and violent behavior.

The Poisonwood Bible

Barbara Kingsolver

One of my all time favorites. A great book if you enjoy alterenating narrators.

Siddhartha

Hermann Hesse

Not a huge fan- struck me as a "Enlightenment for Dummies". If this book were a person, he's the type to not wear shoes and think he's better than you for it.

Flowers in the Attic

V. C. Andrews

Creepy. I would argue that the distrubing themes are sensationalized and not appropriately conveyed the author, especially considering it appears to have a younger target audience. I don't shy away from dark topics, but do so when they read more like a fantasy.

Vincent by himself (Vincent Van Goph Letters)

Bruce Bernard

It always breaks my heart to think of the mental suffering Vincent endured, and how he died never knowing how much and for how long his art would be appreciated.

If I had to pick a conspiracy theory to believe in, it would be the one where Vincent is accidentally shot by a local child instead of by his own hand.

Fun fact: Hypothetically, if I could have dinner with 5 people (dead or alive, real or fictitious), Vincent is one I think of. See also, Charles Dickens.

A Doll's House

Henrik Ibsen

It's interesting how progressive Ibsen was regarding female gender roles and societal expectations for a man born in 1828.

Go Set A Watchman

Harper Lee

I was told this book would ruin To Kill a Mockingbird (TKAM). I think it was an interesting insight into the development of TKAM. How did Harper Lee publish one of the most iconic books ever and then just dip? I always think about the (disproved) rumors that Capote really was behind TKAM, but Lee stated she didn't want to try to measure up to the success of TKAM with another novel.

Great Expectations

Charles Dickens

One of my favorites of all time. Joe and Pip's relationship is one of my favorite literary relationships. I really liked the characters and how all their stories intertwined. You should also watch the movie adaptation with Douglas Booth in it.

Trifles

Susan Glaspell

The subtleness is amazing.

True Grit

Charles Portis

Maddie is such an enjoyable character, and I like the cadence of the writing style.
