Lord of the Flies

William Golding

Controversial but this book is awful and I stand by that. It's an unrealistic, pessemestic, clunky, and one-dimensional flawed allegory that makes the fatal mistake of not including female characters partially because, in Golding's own words (source: preface of his own narration of the audiobook), sex would have had to belong in the story (of 6 to 12 year olds). Has Golding ever met a child, a woman, or a person? People and society are far more complex than he gives credit for, and the points he tries to push are not dissimilar to an edgy 12 year old posting on Facebook "humans are just animals in suits".

Lolita

Vladimir Nabokov

The prose is spectular; a bitter, disgusting tragedy unreliably narrated by a charming perpetrator.

Unfortunately the film adaptations and publishing industry (misleading covers) failed in portraying the complexity of the novel, leading to horrific romantization of abhorrent themes.

Personally I think it was Nabokov's goal to show how eloquent some monsters can be and how depraved we all are for falling for it, especially when you consider claims by some that Nabokov himself may have been sexually abused by his wealthy uncle.

SPOILER: It's always been my take that Humbert murdered Charlotte. Nabokov wouldn't be so sloppy with his plot to make a convinient death like that, and Humbert constantly fantasized about her death in very specific ways. The ambiguity is part of the appeal though.

I could write a lot in this review, but thinking about the book and society's reception of it for too long makes me want to throw up.

As I Lay Dying

William Faulkner

Absolutely amazing, genuis execution of stream of consciousness and symbolism. Every single line has at least one but usually several words that individually strike you as masterfully chosen.

Faulkner has an almost inhuman sense of time and space; seriously consider this quote in the context of Einstein's spacetime continuum:

"It is as though the space between us were time: an irrevocable quality. It is as though time, no longer running straight before us in a diminishing line, now runs parallel between us like a looping string, the distance being the doubling accretion of the thread and not the interval between."

The James Franco film adaptation was great as well.

In my top 5 favorites (maybe, it's hard to pick). Somehow the Sound and the Fury is even better though.

"My mother is a fish."
